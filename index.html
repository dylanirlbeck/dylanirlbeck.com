Hi, I'm Dylan.

I'm currently a software engineer at Flexport, where I'm helping to build the operating system for global trade (I work specifically in the consolidations and warehousing domains). I'm also a proud alumni and recent graduate of the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign (UIUC). (You can find recent highlights of my work here.)

When able, I spend time thinking about how to construct robust, maintainable, and equitable digital systems. From an engineering perspective, I'm presently interested in how to combine domain-driven design and functional programming to build the "right" software. At the same time, I'm trying to explore where so many of our current digital systems went wrong (particularly via more focus on technology policy and computing ethics), and how to avoid those same pitfalls in my work. Finally, I'm very interested in government and civic technology as avenues to build truly impactful software: if you're doing work in these fields and see an opportunity to collaborate with me, I'd love to hear from you.

Previously, I have worked on improving internal documentation with the General Services Adminstration (as a Coding it Forward Civic Digital Fellow); advocated for -- and began building -- a more robust computing ethics curriculum at UIUC; and developed software for a number of Chicago startups and organizations.
